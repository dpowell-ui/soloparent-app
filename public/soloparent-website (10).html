The Mission

The best thing you can do for your kids is not do this alone.

Research is clear: children in single-parent homes face greater risk of instability, disconnection, and struggle. But those outcomes aren't inevitable — they change when the parent has support.

That's the whole mission. When a single parent finds real community, honest resources, and people who truly get it — they show up differently for their kids. More present. More grounded. More whole. Solo Parent exists to make that happen, for free, for every single parent who needs it.

The Society Pathway

Every month has a path. You don't have to find it alone.

Inside the Society, each month follows a clear pathway — from a single podcast conversation all the way through to a live town hall. Every step is intentional, every piece connects, and it all moves you forward.

🎙️
The Anchor
The Podcast Sets the Theme
Each month opens with a new podcast episode that anchors the theme — an honest, expert-led conversation about what single parents are actually navigating right now. This is where the month begins.
Weekly · Every Monday
🤝
The Community
Society Circles Pick It Up
The podcast episode becomes the conversation inside that week's Circles. Single parents show up, hear each other's perspectives, and move from passive listening to active processing — together.
Daily Circles · 7 Days a Week
🌅
The Daily Thread
Daily Check-Ins Reinforce the Theme
Every morning, a short check-in lands in your inbox or on your phone — a reflection, a prompt, or an encouragement that keeps the month's theme alive between sessions. Small but consistent.
Every Morning · App · Text · Email
📓
The Practice
Resources & Prompts Go Deeper
Journal prompts, reflection guides, and practical resources tied to the theme are available all month. Designed for the 10 minutes between school pickup and dinner — not a 3-hour course.
On Demand · All Month
🏛️
The Culmination
Town Hall Closes the Loop
At the end of each month, a live Town Hall brings the whole community together — Robert Beeson, Elizabeth Cole, and a guest go deeper on the theme, answer real questions, and close the month with momentum for the next.
Monthly · Live & Recorded

Our Story

It started with one man who couldn't find help anywhere.

Robert Beeson was a Grammy Award–winning music executive with what looked like a full life. Then his wife left, and he suddenly found himself alone raising three daughters — searching desperately for resources that simply didn't exist.

So he built them. Starting with a single support group in Franklin, TN that filled up without a single announcement from the pulpit, Robert grew Solo Parent into a national movement. 220,000+ families. 18 states. The US military. International online groups.

Today Solo Parent shows up for single parents seven days a week — because Robert knows firsthand what it feels like when no one does.

"I quickly realized there were very few resources for single parents like me. I created Solo Parent so no one has to navigate this alone."
— Robert Beeson, Founder & CEO
